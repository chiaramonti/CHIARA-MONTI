Good morning! We are Ghirotti Benedetta and Monti Chiara, students of Language Society and Communication at the University of Bologna. The aim of our project was to explore the ArCo dataset in order to enrich the information related to archaeological parks, which served as the topic of interest for our research.

The Italian ArCo database served as the foundation for our project, which seeks to enrich and expand its coverage. Our attention was directed specifically toward archaeological parks across Italy, exploring their historical, cultural, and architectural relevance. These sites are essential in preserving national heritage and provide a vital resource for academic research into ancient civilizations, urban planning, and societal development.

To evaluate how comprehensively ArCo classifies these archaeological parks, we designed a series of SPARQL queries to rigorously interrogate the dataset.

Throughout this evaluation, several omissions and areas lacking in detail became apparent. In response, we employed Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance our understanding of the subject. Insights generated by the LLMs (ChatGPT and Gemini) were subsequently converted into new triples, enriching the ArCo database with more nuanced and comprehensive information.

The project was conducted with a collaborative and coordinated approach, ensuring a comprehensive understanding across all phases and strategically organized efforts. Joint reviews guaranteed coherence and a shared, in-depth comprehension of the overall process.

Initially, collective decision-making for the topic proved challenging due to extensive existing documentation in the ARCO knowledge base, leading to a pivot toward archaeological parks.

Analyzing available ARCO data allowed us to identify key areas for enrichment. This exploratory phase was followed by complementary and coordinated work on both content and technical structure, culminating in a cohesive and effective final product fully aligned with our initial goals.
