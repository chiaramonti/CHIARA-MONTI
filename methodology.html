Methodology

This second section is devoted to a detailed explanation of the methodology adopted throughout the project.

As an initial step, we identified a clearly defined area of focus that aligned with our research interests. For the purposes of this study, we chose to concentrate on Archaeological Sites (as already mentioned in the home page), given their historical significance and relevance within the broader context of cultural heritage.

Accordingly, we began our research within the ArCo dataset by designing and executing the following SPARQL queries.

SPARQL Query Result 
PREFIX rdf: <http://www.w3.org/1999/02/22-rdf-syntax-ns#>
PREFIX rdfs: <http://www.w3.org/2000/01/rdf-schema#>
PREFIX arco: <https://w3id.org/arco/ontology/arco/>

SELECT DISTINCT ?cp ?label ?depiction
WHERE {
  ?cp rdfs:label ?label ;
      rdf:type arco:CulturalProperty .
  FILTER(REGEX(?label , "parco archeologico" , "i"))
  OPTIONAL { ?cp foaf:depiction ?depiction }
}
ORDER BY (?label)
LIMIT 100
The query retrieves up to 100 cultural properties that are labeled as “parco archeologico”, along with their labels and any available images, sorted alphabetically by name.

However, the results of this query were too general for our investigation, so we decided to formulate another query that investigated the class ArchaeologicalParks.

Subsequently, in order to enrich the data related to the archaeological parks we had selected, we decided to consult ChatGPT, which provided us with the following response. To formulate this question, we employed the zero-shot prompting technique.

By reviewing the results provided by ChatGPT and comparing them with the information already available in the ArCo database regarding the archaeological parks we selected, we have decided to add three new triples—currently missing—for each park.

The first triple concerns the precise geographical coordinates of the selected archaeological parks, expressed in terms of latitude and longitude. For this, we will use the predicate geo:lat/geo:long, already present in ArCo.

The second triple refers to the conservation status of the parks, a fundamental aspect in this context. We will introduce new triples using the ArCo predicate a-dd:ConservationStatusType.

The third triple we propose involves introducing links to equivalent entities in external datasets. We have chosen to work with Wikidata. This new triple will use the predicate owl:sameAs, which is also already included in ArCo.

RESEARCH FOR THE SECOND TRIPLE

Subsequently, we focused on proposing the insertion of a triple related to the conservation status. ChatGPT's response had provided us with an interesting insight on the general condition, but we aimed to include the type of conservation status, which required a more specific predicate.

For this reason, using the technique of zero-shot prompting, we asked both ChatGPT and Gemini which label was used in ARCO to indicate the type of conservation status, providing as an example the label used in ARCO to indicate the material. Presented below are the question posed to the LLMs and their corresponding responses.

Q: Given that on ArCo the property for material is arco-dd:hasMaterial, what is the property for the conservation status type of cultural properties?

A:

ChatGPT Gemini
The property for the conservation status type of cultural properties in ArCo is arco-dd:hasConservationStatusType.

This property is used to specify the type of conservation status (e.g., “good”, “poor”, “restored”) within the conservation status description, which itself is linked to the cultural property via arco-dd:hasConservationStatus.

So the full path is:

Cultural property → arco-dd:hasConservationStatus → Conservation Status → arco-dd:hasConservationStatusType → Conservation Status Type (e.g., a concept from a controlled vocabulary).

 On ArCo, the property used to describe the conservation status of cultural properties is arco-dd:hasConservationStatus.

This property links a cultural property to an instance of arco-dd:ConservationStatus, which then has a core:hasType property that specifies the arco-dd:ConservationStatusType (e.g., GoodConservationStatus, MediocreConservationStatus, etc.).

So, the full path to get to the type of conservation status would typically be:

CulturalProperty arco-dd:hasConservationStatus ?conservationStatusInstance. ?conservationStatusInstance core:hasType ?conservationStatusType .
Evaluation: The clarity of this LLM's response is undeniable, providing the sought-after answer promptly before delving into comprehensive details. It is worth noting, however, that it does not include, despite not being explicitly requested, any illustrative examples of the conservation status types available on ArCo. Evaluation: Unlike the former, this LLM's response does not proceed directly to the desired information. Instead, it first presents a broader property, only then disclosing the specific property that was requested. Moreover, it includes several illuminating examples, which are beneficial for grasping the array of conservation status types found in ArCo.

It is now necessary to focus on determining the conservation status of the Parco archeologico di Saturo.

Despite announced restoration works and projects (such as those restarted in October 2024, with inauguration initially planned for September 2024), the current situation of the Archaeological Park of Saturo presents severe critical issues. The reopening was limited to a single day (June 19, 2024) for guided tours of the construction site, and as of today (June 2025), a simple Google search reveals the park is temporarily closed and inaccessible to tourists.

Furthermore, research from 2022 indicates that the Park was overrun by weeds, posing a high risk of fires due to high temperatures, and showed remains of food and used beverages. Unauthorised crossings of the cliff through the Park were also observed, with archaeological remains being trampled, even by bicycles, highlighting a serious lack of control and protection.

Notwithstanding the efforts and funding for ongoing restoration work, the actual usability, safety, and protection of the Parco archeologico di Saturo are severely compromised. The continued closure to the public and persistent management and degradation issues confirm a classification of BadConservationStatus, confirming ChatGPT evaluation.

Regarding the third triple, our aim was to insert a link connecting our ArCo resource to an equivalent or nearly equivalent resource on Wikidata.
